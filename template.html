The internet: Not designed for people with disabilities. [example of trying to use some site with a screenreader, failing] [show the same site, now with mouse to demonstrate the difference?]
A lot of sites have problems like that. How can we fix them? This is a complex problem, not caused by a single root issue, so we have to find a way to start solving it, then go from there. The starting point is, for lack of a better term, to convince your boss. Your situation likely varies, so you may have to convince your coworkers, your client or just yourself. But the point is the same: You have to convince someone that making websites accessible is important, just like user experience and security are important. Once we agree on the importance, we can look at testing for accessibility and fixing issues as we find them.

Convince Your Boss
There’s laws to consider, these apply specifically when working on government sites.

Legal Reasons

Applies to government sites.

Legal reasons

  • USA: Section 508
  • DE: Grundgesetz, Artikel 3 Abs. 3 Satz 2: “Niemand darf wegen seiner Behinderung benachteiligt werden”. More specific: BGG Also BITV
  • AT: BGStG and E-GovG
  • UK: PAS-78 and BS8878
  • More tom come in the next decades, since most states have signed the 2006 UN Convention on the Rights of Persons with Disabilities
Making web sites accessible can make a huge difference for those who benefit the most from technology. Someone who can’t leave his home benefits a lot from being able to shop online.

Commcercial Reasons

When you can't leave home, shopping online is so much more useful.
There’s an ethical side. As the Golden Rule says: “One should treat others as one would like others to treat oneself.” Imagine you have an accident and become blind yourself. Wouldn’t you want the internet to be accessible to you as well? [also, everyone gets old, which heavily increases the probablity for disabilities]

Ethical Reasons

Golden Rule: “One should treat others as one would like others to treat oneself.”
There’s side effects: Making your site easier to use for disabled users is likely going to have an effect on everyone else as well. Keyboard support is useful for anyone who doesn’t want or can use the mouse, could be a disability or superusers.
Working with clean markup is good for screenreaders, but also has other benefits, for SEO or preview snippets (Twitter, Facebook?).

Semantic Reasons

Good markup and separation of concerns has other benefits
If nothing else works, go guerilla and “ask for forgiveness, not permission”: Do some testing and improvements, then show how cheap a few key improvements are.
